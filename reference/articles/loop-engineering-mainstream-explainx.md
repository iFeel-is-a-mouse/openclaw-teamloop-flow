## Summary

In June 2026, "loop engineering" became the single most-discussed AI development topic on developer Twitter. The term was sparked by Peter Steinberger's post suggesting developers should stop prompting coding agents and instead "design loops that prompt your agents." 

The concept was then named and codified by Google Chrome DevRel lead Addy Osmani in his blog post, and gained decisive endorsement from Anthropic Claude Code lead Boris Cherny, who stated: "I don't prompt Claude anymore. I have loops running that prompt Claude and figuring out what to do. My job is to write loops."
